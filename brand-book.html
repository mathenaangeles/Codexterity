02 Logo system

One wordmark. Two contexts.

The mark is fully typographic — no separate icon required. The lowercase "co" carries the accent color, and the "x" is cut with a single angled accent that nods to code brackets and to dexterity. Use it exactly as supplied; never rebuild it.

Primary · on #000000
Light · on #F1EFE9
Minimum clearspace = height of the lowercase "o" on all sides · min digital width 96px · min print 22mm
Do
  • Use the lime mark only on true black or near-black
  • Use the cobalt mark on the paper / light neutral
  • Keep it horizontal, unedited, from the supplied files
  • Give it room — respect clearspace on every side
Don't
  • Recolor the accent outside Volt or Cobalt
  • Rebuild, redraw, or re-letter the wordmark
  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or add shadows / bevels
  • Place it on busy imagery without a dark scrim

04 Typography

Garet for statements. Inter for speed.

Garet's geometric, softly-cut forms carry warmth without losing precision — the voice of headlines and the wordmark. Inter is the workhorse for UI, body, and data. Garet is a licensed commercial face; on-screen specimens fall back to Sora where Garet isn't installed.

Pairing rule

Never set Garet below 20px — it loses personality and fights Inter. Never let Inter carry a headline above 32px — it flattens beside the wordmark's geometry. One display line, Inter for everything supporting it.

Tracking & case

Headlines: sentence case, tight tracking (-1.5 to -3.5%). Eyebrows & labels: uppercase mono, +6 to +14% tracking. Body: never uppercase, never justified.

07 Iconography & imagery

Line-based, systemic, never literal.

Single-weight strokes on a 1.6px grid. No robot heads, no circuit-board brains, no "AI sparkle." Imagery favors real interfaces, dashboards, and abstract texture over stock photos of people pointing at holograms.

Texture & backgrounds

Dark surfaces carry fine film-grain (3–5% opacity), hairline grid or dot textures masked into softness, and low-opacity radial glows in Volt and Cobalt. Never a hard gradient fill, never particle fields or glowing brains.

Gradient type

Reserve gradient fills for one or two key words per view — Volt into Cobalt on display type, as an accent, not a default. The rest of the type stays solid white or black for legibility.
